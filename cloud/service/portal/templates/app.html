The Kyro app

Emergency brain-injury care, offline.

Kyro is an on-device co-pilot for acute traumatic brain injury in low-resource settings. It guides a general medical officer through a structured assessment and a guideline-backed decision, with every recommendation tied to a named source. It runs on an ordinary Android phone, with no internet and no scanner.

{# ---- what it does ---- #}
What it does

Built for the room with no neurosurgeon

Works with no signal

The whole decision runs on the device. No internet, no cloud, no continuous power. It is designed to run on phones that are eight to ten years old.

Structured assessment

It drives a disciplined work-up: GCS, pupils, blood pressure, lucid interval, mechanism, and time since injury. You can answer hands-free by voice.

Guideline-backed decisions

It follows a clinical decision tree to a recommendation on stabilization, preventing secondary injury, and the operate-versus-transfer call.

Every claim is cited

Each recommendation shows the source it came from, such as the Brain Trauma Foundation guidelines or the Peshawar Recommendations, and flags when it is inferring.

Honest about its limits

Help is badged green, amber, or red. On the steps that need imaging or a specialist, Kyro stops and connects a live human expert instead of guessing.

A dropped call loses nothing

Kyro holds the full state of the encounter. If a teleconsult drops mid-emergency, reconnecting rebuilds a pre-briefed handoff so the expert is caught up at once.

Knowledge base

10,172 nodes, pre-loaded and offline

Kyro ships pre-configured with a signed knowledge base of 10,172 nodes, built from the guidelines and references below. It sits on the phone before the app ever sees a signal, so a recommendation never waits on a network. Knowledge from the field syncs in when a connection exists, and the pre-loaded core is always enough on its own.

{# ---- how it decides ---- #}
How it decides

Most help the evidence supports, badged honestly

🟢 Protocol

The decision tree reached a guideline-backed answer and the knowledge base covers it. Kyro acts, with citations.

🟡 Grounded guidance

The exact case is not in the tree, so Kyro gives the best guidance its cited knowledge supports, clearly labeled as extrapolated rather than validated.

🔴 Stop

For steps that need imaging, like where to place a burr hole, or for contradictory input, Kyro stops and connects a live expert.
