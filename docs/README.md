These docs capture everything the team has worked out so far, so anyone can get up to speed fast. Written 2026-06-19.

6. **[06-tech-stack.md](06-tech-stack.md)** — the concrete 2026 tooling per layer (on-device LLM, ASR, TTS, MT, GraphRAG, mobile runtime, cloud build plane, escalation), with the weekend-MVP subset.
7. **[07-kyro-product-and-pitch.md](07-kyro-product-and-pitch.md)** — **the product, now named "Kyro": context & current framing** — the name, the *"continuity, not knowledge"* thesis, the procedure state machine, validation, business model, competitors, and the demo. (A context doc, not a locked pitch.)
8. **[08-knowledge-sources.md](08-knowledge-sources.md)** — **the data behind Kyro:** the master sourcing catalog for the knowledge graph, organized by trust tier (Tier 0 canonical core vs. Tier 1 enrichment) — TBI guidelines, the EDH burr-hole procedure spine, the no-neurosurgeon LMIC consensus, operative atlases/video libraries, named authorities, lecture series, and validation datasets. Includes the recommended ingestion priority for the weekend MVP bundle.
9. **[09-academic-library.md](09-academic-library.md)** — **Layer 2 of the knowledge pyramid (core):** the academic harvest — institutional/government references, lectures + slides + courseware, primary literature + official guidelines, operative technique + atlases + anatomy + simulation + **surgical instruments**. 10. **[10-clinical-management-library.md](10-clinical-management-library.md)** — **Layer 2 (clinical):** managing the patient around the OR — decision rules & scoring (GCS-P, CCHR, CRASH/IMPACT, Marshall/Rotterdam), anesthesia/airway, post-op & complications, special populations — and the **hard-coded safety rules** to encode first.
11. **[11-context-and-journey-library.md](11-context-and-journey-library.md)** — **Layer 2 (context & journey):** foundational neuroanatomy, plus the case arc around surgery — prevention/education, prehospital recognition/transport/triage, diagnosis-without-CT (ONSD/POCUS/NIRS/GFAP), and rehabilitation.
12. **[12-data-business-competitive-library.md](12-data-business-competitive-library.md)** — **Layer 2 (data & business):** datasets (CQ500, MIMIC-IV, registries), global-surgery/health-systems/policy, the competitor & prior-art landscape, and named experts/courses/podcasts.
13. **[13-lmic-context.md](13-lmic-context.md)** — **Layer 3 of the pyramid — the LMIC foundation:** who LMICs are and how care is done *differently* than in the West. Built around a "Western default vs LMIC reality vs Kyro response" contrast, covering classification/demographics, health systems, infrastructure (power/connectivity/transport), economics/financing, culture & care-seeking, frugal innovation & why Western tech fails, the Pakistan/Gilgit-Baltistan/Namibia deep dive, and equity/ethics — plus design/pitch implications and a key-facts cheat sheet.
14. **[14-peshawar-pakistan-context.md](14-peshawar-pakistan-context.md)** — **Layer 4 — Peshawar/KP, the Pakistani neurotrauma reality:** built on two repo primary sources (Dr. Nawaz Khan's Lady Reading Hospital lecture + the verbatim Peshawar Recommendations) plus a 5-agent harvest. How they *actually* manage TBI, their stated #1 concern (broken referral), Peshawar capacity (LRH/HMC/KTH), the RTA+blast/firearm casemix, EDH outcomes & the golden window *validated in Pakistani data*, the KP health system/Sehat Card, and the key people/policy — with a "make it effective in Pakistan" synthesis.
15. **[15-gmo-and-patient.md](15-gmo-and-patient.md)** — **Layer 5 — the two people in the case:** a deep profile of the **GMO** (training, near-zero neuro operative experience, the JIT-decision-support evidence, why teleconsult fails) and the **patient HM** (the Three Delays, wait-times/golden-window numbers, and the care gaps he fell through — incl. GB's zero ICU beds and the rehab/epilepsy/financial-catastrophe burden), with a GMO+patient → Kyro design synthesis.
16. **[16-gap-analysis.md](16-gap-analysis.md)** — **the red-team:** an honest, prioritized inventory of every gap/weakness/blind-spot across the whole pyramid (clinical, technical, regulatory, liability, business, validation, framing, evidence-integrity), severity-ranked 🔴/🟠/🟡, synthesized from docs 01–15 + an external pressure-test of the riskiest assumptions (RAG-doesn't-fix-hallucination, surgical-device regulation, cognitive-aid safety limits, on-device-LLM risk, LMIC digital-health sustainability). Ends with what to fix first.
17. **[17-gap-remediation.md](17-gap-remediation.md)** — **how to fix the gaps:** a direct companion to doc 16 turning every gap into a concrete fix tagged [REFRAME] (free, do now), [DESIGN] (build into the MVP), or [ROADMAP] (acknowledge honestly). Ends with the 6 highest-priority fixes in order.
18. **[18-analogous-cases.md](18-analogous-cases.md)** — **real cases like HM's:** news + peer-reviewed cases that mirror the patient — the near-perfect analog (Dr. Rob Carson's household-drill, phone-guided EDH evacuation, Australia 2009), the "talk-and-die" cautionary cases (Natasha Richardson), non-neurosurgeon burr holes in remote/austere settings, LMIC non-specialist EDH evacuation with outcomes (Uganda/Cambodia/Tanzania), and the closest geographic analog (Ladakh, a high-altitude general-surgeon neurotrauma series). With a pitch synthesis.
19. **[19-test-vignettes.md](19-test-vignettes.md)** — **the evaluation benchmark:** 38 real-world-grounded test vignettes (similar to HM but not identical) to measure Kyro's accuracy *and* resilience. Four categories — **A. in-scope EDH → GUIDE** (12), **B. borderline → OBSERVE** (7), **C. gap/out-of-scope → ABSTAIN** (14), **D. complex/harm-trap** (5) — each with an answer-key action, expected Kyro mode, what it tests, real-case grounding, and the "generic-LLM trap." Drives the **three-arm comparison** (Kyro ≫ generic-LLM ≫ unaided), the **ablation** (with/without spine+abstention), the **live refusal demo**, and **mentor sign-off**; scored on 3 metrics (action accuracy, **harm rate target 0%**, abstention rate on gap cases).

- **Phase:** converging on a named product (**Kyro**); MVP scoped, not yet built.
- **Leading pain point:** Surgical Task-Shifting (case pain point #6) — specifically the moment the GMO's teleconsult call kept dropping during HM's emergency burr-hole.
- **Leading idea — "Kyro":** an offline, on-device **GraphRAG** surgical co-pilot whose core innovation is a **procedure state machine** that never loses its place — so *the call can drop and nothing is lost*. Source-cited; ships with a canonical core; grows via a curated expert network. Sharpened thesis: **the problem isn't knowledge, it's continuity.**
- **Architecture:** specified in [05-architecture.md](05-architecture.md) — offline-first; "knowledge as versioned data, not model weights"; one provenance-tagged KG; sneakernet-friendly sync; patient data never leaves the device.
- **Edge-of-knowledge behavior** (doc 05): when the KB has a gap the product degrades gracefully (stabilize-and-transfer, never invents), self-heals via expert "knowledge requests" (open-source-issues-style), and escalates *critical* cases to a live matched expert over WhatsApp. This is arguably our strongest trust signal for the AI-skeptic panel.
- **Validation:** mentor neurosurgeon sign-off (this weekend) + MIMIC-4 concordance study + Namibia GMO design partners.
- **Business:** Kyro = Exo's philanthropic wing; NGO/ministry/grant funding; beachhead Namibia; Mission:Brain's 130 chapters as the contributor seed. (Starlink/hardware = future roadmap, not core.)
- **Open decision (whole team):** how hard to lean on the "AI" framing given an AI-skeptic neurosurgeon judging panel (doc 04's three paths), and how tightly to gate expert uploads (doc 05).

- **5-minute pitch** + 2 min Q&A. Six categories × 5 pts = 30: (1) problem clarity, (2) innovation, (3) business model/launch, (4) validation/MVP, (5) presentation, (6) inspiration.
- **Hard constraints from the case:** unreliable internet, intermittent electricity, minimal trained staff, extreme poverty, geographic/cultural barriers.
